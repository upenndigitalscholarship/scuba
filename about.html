Scholarly Communication UnBoxed Activities

Why SCUBA?

The Big Thinking Reason: To broaden the conversation about the role of libraries in scholarly communication and the technologies we use; to dream up as many great ideas as possible for how we might tackle a set of sticky problems.

The Practical Reason: Most institutions are offering scholarly communications services, platforms, and tools, though the specifics are quite variable. Our needs overlap, but we often differ in our approaches and priorities. This is an opportune time to learn from one another.

Introduction

SCUBA provides a kit that is designed to spark conversation across the library community. The kit includes:

1) A set of group activity templates that local organizers can select from. These activity templates are designed to help people think creatively about the role of institutional repositories in the library landscape and to take practical steps to assess their own repositories and plan for changes.

2) Instructions for planning a local SCUBA event, and for sharing the outcomes of that event with the broader community.

Planning an Event

  • Step 1: Determine the scope - who should be involved? What do you hope to accomplish?
  • Step 2: Identify which activities you’ll do
  • Step 3: Plan and hold the event and collect feedback
  • Step 4: Refine feedback and report back to the larger community
  • Step 5: Repeat as needed

Local planning for SCUBA would begin with a group of people (likely from the same institution or consortium) getting together to do a small about of pre-work. They should identify a local goal and identify ways to get participants into the right frame of mind for this activity. The group should then select a sub-set of activities from the Box to work on together. After working on the activities, the group should continue their discussion and then report back to the larger community about what they learned and what their next steps will be. For larger events, a coordinator or coordinating committee might map out the event plans in advance and lead the larger group through.

Activities

The activities available will include options that will fall into one of three categories: Practical Activities, Dreamy Activities, and Conference Contributions.

Practical Activities are aimed at getting some of the “busy” work done that is needed to plan for changes to institutional repositories. These include writing institutional or platform profiles based on the templates we’ve developed for the Beprexit environmental scan, and defining scholarly communication terms for the larger community to encourage a shared understanding of the words we use as we discuss institutional repositories.

Dreamy Activities will help communities think more broadly about what institutional repositories could be, and what they are missing. They will include activities for speculating about the possibilities at your institution, based on people and resources, and for creatively rethinking the scope and functions of your repository.

Conference Contributions will also be welcome to expand the set of activities that make the kit.
